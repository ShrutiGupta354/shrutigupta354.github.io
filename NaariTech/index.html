Ruchi Sanghvi

Ruchi Sanghvi is an Indian computer engineer and businesswoman. She was the first female engineer hired by Facebook. In 2016, Sanghvi established South Park Commons, a residential and professional tech space that functions similarly to a hackerspace.

Elizabeth Varley

Elizabeth’s team works with corporates to help them with innovation culture and offers free business growth support to underrepresented founders in tech. Reshma Saujani is a leading activist and the founder of Girls Who Code and the Marshall Plan for Moms. She has spent more than a decade building movements to fight for women and girls’ economic empowerment, working to close the gender gap in the tech sector, and most recently advocating for policies to support moms impacted by the pandemic.

About Me

Hi! I'm Shruti and here is my story.

I am a first-generation college student currently pursuing a degree at Walsh University in Ohio, USA. I was born and raised in Nepal, and during high school, I took my first computer science class out of curiosity and discovered my passion for coding. In 2019, I came to the United States to pursue my undergraduate degree in Mathematics and Computer Science.
Despite being the only girl in my high school class to pursue a career in computer science, I was determined to access more opportunities during my undergraduate years. I applied to several universities in the United States and was fortunate enough to receive a scholarship at Walsh University. I was excited to make friends with similar interests, work on coding projects together, and establish a "Women in CS" club on campus. However, I soon discovered that I was the only female student in my higher-level computer science and mathematics classes, a story shared by students at other universities across the United States. This made me realize that this issue is not limited to underdeveloped and developing countries but exists worldwide, including in the United States. However, some developed nations have organizations that aim to increase women's participation in technology and make progress, while many developing countries, such as Nepal, still lack such opportunities.
The problem is that many women are unaware of the resources available to them, especially those tailored to women. In fact, I myself was unaware of these resources until my junior year of college when I spent hours scouring the internet for them. I do not want you to experience the same struggles. Hence, I have created this website, which serves as a "library" of all types of resources. I will continuously gather information such as a list of scholarships available for women in technology, organizations working on empowering women in STEM, YouTubers who provide free coding lessons, websites for learning computer science for free, and more. This website will provide all of this collected information for free. The goal is to provide as much information as possible so that you do not have to spend hours searching for them online.
